We are seeking members

Nakuja project is a student-driven project. Most of the students come from Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture And Technology (JKUAT). There are also students from other universities, such as Kenyatta University and Dedan Kimathi University of Technology.


Internship

The best way to catch up with the team is to join the internship program. In 2024, we will offer the internship for 12 weeks from January to April. We will offer another batch from September to December 2024 for the same 12 weeks.

The internship program is open to all students in Kenya, but we need commitment for the whole period. This is not suitable for the students who intend just to fulfill the requirements for their curriculum. It is an intensive program, and the participants will learn a lot from interacting with the other participants.

The students are also expected to learn by themselves actively. The participants are recommended to have the following practical skills as much as possible before joining the program.

  • Mechanical design, 3D CAD (SolidWorks), ANSYS (FEM and CFD)
  • Manual machining (lathe, drilling), CNC machining, Welding, 3D printing
  • Circuit design, Embedded programming (Arduino, ESP32, Raspberry Pi), Oscilloscope
  • Antenna design, Vector network analyzer, Web programming
  • C/C++, Python, MATLAB, FreeRTOS, Linux

For those who don't have sufficient knowledge and skills yet, it is recommended to apply after you have learned the basics.


Final year project

For JKUAT students, the final year student can conduct their final year project under the Nakuja project. Below is the list of the final year projects that were done in the previous years.

  • Design and development of a liquid rocket engine closed loop control cooling system
  • Design and fabrication of an active fin control system for a rocket
  • Design and development of a controlled plumbing system for a liquid rocket engine
  • Design and development of a fuel injector for a liquid engine rocket
  • Development of a thrust vector control system for a Nakuja model rocket
